Design Manifesto © Peggy Cheng 2024
01

Design
with
Context

Emerging technologies in museums act as translators, bridging the gap between audiences and the content.

Interactive media should seamlessly integrate into exhibitions, guided by visitor flow and the overarching narrative.

These technologies enhance accessibility while providing contextual interpretations that deepen understanding.

02

Meaningful Interaction
Beyond Technology

Design emerging technologies to prioritize visitor experiences, not the technology itself.

Every interaction should serve a purpose.
Prioritize storytelling and audience insights to provoke thought, reflection, and deep engagement.

Place technologies strategically to resonate with audiences at the right moment, ensuring they complement the story rather than overshadow it.

03

Build Stories
That
Resonate

Anchor exhibitions in their social, historical, and cultural contexts to create meaningful connections.

Use audience research—through interviews, surveys, and testing—to ensure exhibits resonate with visitors' experiences and cultural backgrounds.

Collaborate with historians, sociologists, scientists and cultural experts to ensure accuracy and depth, enhancing the relevance and impact of interactive designs.

04

Seeing the World
Through New Lenses

Empower visitors to see the world through new perspectives by acting as facilitators of insight.

Use tools like thematic visuals, storytelling, and immersive audio tours to help audiences "look through the lens" of the subject matter.

Immersive media should foster empathy and connection by embodying the experiences of others, allowing visitors to step into different realities.

05

Innovate Boldly

Push the boundaries of design by embracing emerging technologies such as AR, VR, and AI.

Leverage these tools to make the invisible visible, expand storytelling possibilities, and create transformative experiences.

Design should address today's needs while anticipating tomorrow's opportunities. Experiment boldly to shape the future of how audiences experience art, history, culture and all forms of knowledge.

06

Explore Through
Play

Foster curiosity and discovery through playful, gamified design elements.

Create sensory-rich environments that inspire interaction and experimentation while balancing dynamic experiences with contemplative moments for reflection.

07

Accessibility
&
Inclusivity First

Accessibility is not an afterthought but a foundational element of design.

Prioritize accessibility by incorporating multilingual, tactile, and auditory elements while considering neurodiverse and physically varied audiences.

Accessibility should remove physical, cognitive, and cultural barriers, enabling everyone to engage fully with the exhibition.

08

Foster Communities, Not Just Audiences

Foster community and collaboration through design.

Create spaces and experiences that encourage dialogue and collective participation. Host workshops, co-creation events, and participatory activities to transform visitors into collaborators.

Foster a sense of community that deepens connections with the exhibition's themes and narratives.
